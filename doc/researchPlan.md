# 🧠 RESEARCH PHASE: WHAT YOU’RE LOOKING FOR

You’re not just “finding tools.”

# 🪜 PART 4 — HOW TO APPLY (STEP-BY-STEP)

Once you find a tool:

### Step 1 – Find their affiliate page
Search: `tool name + affiliate program`  
If not found, look for “Partners” or “Affiliates” in the footer.

### Step 2 – Fill out the application
You’ll be asked:
- **Website URL** – if your site is not live yet, say “in development, launching in [month]”. Many programs accept this.
- **Traffic estimate** – be honest (“building content + SEO strategy, currently low but targeted”).
- **Promotion methods** – “content site, comparison articles, social media (Pinterest, TikTok)”

### Step 3 – Position yourself (use this template)

> *“I create comparison‑based content that helps users choose the best tools. I focus on high‑intent traffic and detailed reviews. My audience is [niche audience, e.g., people with bad credit looking to build their score]. I plan to promote your tool in my ‘Best X for Y’ articles and comparison pages.”*

**💡 Suggestion:** If rejected, ask why. Many programs will tell you what they need (e.g., “come back when you have 1,000 monthly visitors”). Keep a list and reapply later.

# 🔗 PART 5 — HOW YOU GET YOUR LINK

Once approved, you’ll receive a **unique tracking link**.

Example:
```
https://tool.com/?ref=yourname123
or
https://tool.com/affiliate?id=12345
```

That’s how they track **your** clicks and sales.

**Trainer Note:** Never share your affiliate link publicly in a way that can be stolen (e.g., on a forum without a redirect). Use your own domain redirects (see Part 6).

---

# 🎭 PART 6 — “DISGUISING” LINKS (IMPORTANT)

Don’t leave raw, ugly links in your articles. They look spammy and hurt trust.

### Option 1 – Simple anchor text (no cloaking)
```html
<a href="https://tool.com/?ref=yourid">Check pricing</a>
```
This is fine for most programs. Just make the anchor text natural.

### Option 2 – Link cloaking (advanced, better for branding)
Use:
- **Pretty Links** (WordPress plugin)
- **Linkly** or **Short.io** (standalone)
- Or build your own redirect in Next.js:  
  Create a page `/go/[tool]` that does a server‑side redirect to the affiliate link.

Example:
```
yourwebsite.com/go/creditstrong → redirects to affiliate link
```

**Why cloak?**  
- Looks cleaner  
- Protects your commissions (some programs allow link theft if raw link is shared)  
- Easy to update the destination without changing every article

# 💳 PART 7 — HOW YOU GET PAID

Each platform has different payment methods and thresholds.

**Common methods:** PayPal, bank transfer (ACH), Stripe, check.

**Minimum payout thresholds:**  
- ShareASale: $25–$50  
- Impact: $50  
- ClickBank: $100 (varies)  
- Amazon: $10 (gift card) or $100 (direct deposit)

**Payment triggers:**
- **CPA (Cost Per Action)** – you get paid when someone signs up (e.g., credit builder app)
- **Revenue share** – you get a percentage of the sale (e.g., 30% of first payment)
- **Recurring** – you get paid every month the customer stays subscribed **(BEST for passive income)**

> **Deep Dive:** Prioritize recurring programs. One customer paying $50/month for a SaaS tool can earn you $15/month forever. After 10 such customers, that’s $150/month residual.

# 📊 PART 8 — WHAT TO LOOK FOR (DON’T SKIP THIS)

When evaluating a potential affiliate tool, ask:

### ✅ GOOD signs:
- $30+ one‑time payout, or 20%+ recurring
- Trusted brand (users recognise it)
- Clear affiliate page with terms
- Dedicated affiliate manager or support
- Easy signup process (no minimum traffic)

### ❌ BAD signs:
- No clear payout information
- Scammy‑looking product (fake reviews, hype language)
- No affiliate support (email bounces)
- Requires huge traffic upfront (10k+ visitors/month)

**Trainer Note:** Start with 3–5 good tools. You don’t need 50 programs. A single high‑performing tool can bring $200–$500/month by itself.
